12.3   Changing Direct to Indirect Speech (Continued)

            Future                             Future in the Past
            “I do not believe the world will   Albert Einstein said (that) he did not believe the world would
            be wiped out in a war fought with   be wiped out in a war fought with the atomic bomb.
            the atomic bomb.”
            Notes
            1. The past forms of modals are usually used in indirect speech (e.g., could, would, might, should, had to).
            2. It is not necessary to change the form of the verb if the situation is still true.
                 The teacher told the students that the earth revolves around the sun.
